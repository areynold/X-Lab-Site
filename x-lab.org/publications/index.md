<p style="line-height: 1.15; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline;">Dear Friends,</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;"><img width="106" height="138" align="right" src="http://action.mdcteam.com/images/Sascha.png" /></span></p>
<p style="line-height: 1.15; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline;">As the 114th Congress convenes, a new crypto war will require steadfast political leadership to ensure our digital rights and our online security are protected. The Internet of Things has opened the floodgates to corporate and government snooping into our most intimate data and activities. Last year, my colleague, <a href="http://sunlightfoundation.com/team/svitka/">Sean Vitka</a>, and I anticipated the scary prospect of a second battle over public cryptography and our fundamental right to control the smart devices that have become an indispensable part of our everyday lives.</span></p>
<p style="line-height: 1.15; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"></p>
<p style="line-height: 1.15; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: medium;"><span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman'; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline;"><br />Crypto War II is the logical next step following the rush by Internet service providers to hang dollars on data traversing their networks -- creating service tiers, interconnection arbitrage, and discriminating amongst services and applications. As distributed infrastructure and end-to-end encryption make it more increasingly difficult for ISPs to know the exact content, pathing, and application being used, there’s a move afoot to shift surveillance from the networks themselves into the devices we use to get online. As this process continues, I worry that ISPs may even try to dissuade users from encryption by relegating encrypted traffic to Internet slow lanes -- in essence, forcing users to trade our privacy for speed.</span></span></p>
<br />
<p style="line-height: 1.15; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"></p>
<p style="line-height: 1.15; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline;">More recently, FBI Director, James Comey, asked Congress to update the Communications Assistance for Law Enforcement Act (CALEA) to force tech companies to build vulnerable backdoors into their devices and services. This government privacy intrusion is couched in the narrative of protecting us from “criminals, terrorists, and child predators,” but CALEA II, as currently conceptualized will actually make us less secure in the long run.</span></p>
<br />
<p style="line-height: 1.15; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"></p>
<p style="line-height: 1.15; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: medium;"><span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman'; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline;">The online world as we know it would not exist without privacy, anonymity, and strong encryption. Sean Vitka</span><span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman'; color: #1155cc; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: underline; vertical-align: baseline;"></span><span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman'; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline;"> and I explore these ideas in our paper -- analyzing how the fight over cryptography and privacy may play out and what we can do to prevent us from heading down a dystopian trajectory. You can read the full article</span><a style="text-decoration: none;" href="https://drive.google.com/file/d/0B0VBrTm1kAktVWRaOXRVZGlXMEk/view?usp=sharing"><span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman'; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline;"> </span><span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman'; color: #1155cc; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: underline; vertical-align: baseline;">here</span></a><span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman'; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline;">.</span></span></p>

<p style="line-height: 1.15; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"></p>
<p style="line-height: 1.15; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: medium;"><span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman'; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline;">Providing critical, honest assessments of tech policy ideas is what </span><a style="text-decoration: none;" href="http://thexlab.org/"><span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman'; color: #1155cc; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: underline; vertical-align: baseline;">X-Lab</span></a><span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman'; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline;"> was designed to do.
